This might strike you as a little counter-intuitive. Losing weight is, after all, one of the most positive health decisions you can make. Most people, however, just stress all the benefits – How wonderful your new life as a healthy, energetic, thin person will be. And it will, to be sure.

There’s More than Fat in Your Fat

Adipose tissue, the medical name for fat, is also where toxins end up stored. They can stay there for years and years, trapped in with your extra pounds.

What kinds of toxins?

Pesticides from fruit and vegetables. Pharmaceutical drugs. Synthetic hormones from dairy. Offgassed plastic from containers. Environmental pollutants from the air and water. Preservatives from processed food. And that’s just a few. Your body is dealing with these things all the time. Whatever can’t be processed by your liver gets packed away into your fat. So what happens when you start to burn up all that fat and lose weight?

All those stored toxins get released. This extra toxic load can overload your liver and cause more problems, disrupt your sleep, make you feel foggy, sluggish, and unfocused. It may not feel good, but it’s actually a good sign! And fortunately, you can do something about it.

The Low Energy Slump

The other risk with weight loss is the low energy slump you can experience. When you begin restricting calorie intake, your body can have a hard time adjusting and you might feel tired, and unmotivated. It might not sound that bad, but these situations are when people start reaching for their habitual quick-fixes: fast carbs, unhealthy snacks, and sugary drinks.

You need clean sources of energy to keep you going through the day and minimize your risk of weight loss sabotage. Any comprehensive weight loss system needs to address these two common pitfalls and plan accordingly.
